About IYEC

Youth-led solutions for a greener world

The International Youth Environmental Challenge (IYEC) is an annual competition dedicated to inspiring and empowering youth to take action on environmental and climate issues for a sustainable future.

This competition unites passionate and innovative young people from around the world to share their ideas addressing local environmental challenges. Participants can present through essays, video presentations, recorded speeches, and other creative formats.

  • ✓Open to individuals or teams worldwide
  • ✓No prior experience required — creativity is enough
  • ✓Judged by industry experts and academics
  • ✓Cash scholarships and certificates awarded
  • ✓Potential to connect with investors and mentors

How to Participate

  • Develop a project on a local environmental issue within one of the themes.
  • Choose your format: essay, speech, video, or other creative media.
  • Fill out the submission form by January 15, 2026.
  • Work individually or as part of a team — your call.
Submit Your Project

Submission Guidelines

  • Must be in written or spoken English, or include English subtitles.
  • Essays: 500–1000 words in Word format.
  • Videos or speeches: 1–5 minutes long.
  • Include your name and category with your submission.
  • Must be original work with proper citations for any quotes.
  • Accessible via a digital link (Google Drive, YouTube, etc.).
  • Identify a local environmental issue and explain your solution.
Judging Criteria

How projects are evaluated

Our panel of experts from academia and industry evaluates every submission across four key dimensions.

01

Innovation

Originality and creativity of the idea. Does it offer a fresh approach to a real problem?

02

Impact

Potential positive impact on the local and global environment. Scale of change possible.

03

Feasibility

Practicality and scalability. Can this solution realistically be implemented?

04

Presentation

Clarity and effectiveness of how the project communicates its ideas and evidence.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The IYEC is open to young people aged 18 and below from around the world — students, young activists, and any individuals or groups interested in addressing environmental and climate challenges. Participants can enter as individuals or as part of a team.
Participants can engage by submitting projects through various formats, including essays, video presentations, recorded speeches, and other creative media. The competition encourages innovative solutions to local and global environmental challenges.
No prior knowledge or experience is required. The IYEC provides a supportive framework to guide participants in developing their projects, making it accessible to all interested youth.
Participation helps build valuable skills and provides a platform for showcasing your ideas. It offers the opportunity to make a meaningful impact on environmental issues, receive recognition, and connect with a global community committed to sustainability. Winners gain exposure to industry experts who may offer advice on implementing initiatives, and well-developed projects might attract potential investors.
There is no preferred method — your creativity is the limit. Whether you present through an essay, video, recorded speech, or another format, use the medium that best showcases your innovative ideas.
Complexity is not a requirement. As the saying goes, "Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication." Simple, well-thought-out solutions can be just as impactful as those involving advanced technology. Focus on creating a practical and effective solution that addresses the environmental issue at hand.
Every problem has a solution, but it might be helpful to narrow your focus. If your issue seems too broad, try breaking it down into smaller, more manageable parts. For instance, while addressing climate change as a whole may be challenging, focusing on reducing pollution in a local pond could be a more achievable goal.
